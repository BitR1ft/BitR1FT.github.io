// About

A brief overview of who I am and what I do

I am a cybersecurity undergraduate at Air University, Islamabad (NCSA Department), currently in my 4th semester of a Bachelor of Science in Cyber Security (BSCYS). My focus lies at the intersection of offensive security and defensive operations, with deep interests in threat detection, SIEM systems, and real-world vulnerability research.

I spend most of my time doing bug bounty research and competing in CTFs under the handle BitR1FT. I have completed multiple full attack chains on HackTheBox machines and have submitted security findings on HackerOne, covering vulnerability classes such as integer overflow, SSRF, and pool redirect attack vectors.

My project work spans both attacking and defending. I have built an autonomous penetration testing platform powered by AI agents, a self-hosted SIEM with custom Sigma detections and threat intel integration, and several security tools for network forensics, malware analysis, and credential management. I am comfortable across both sides of security with a strong emphasis on practical, hands-on skills.
